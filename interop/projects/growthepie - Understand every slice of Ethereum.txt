Fundamental metrics Analysis



Activity

Active Addresses: The number of unique daily addresses that interacted with a chain.
Transaction Count: The number of daily transactions. We try to only count transactions that are executed by users/smart contracts, excluding system transactions.
Throughput: A chain's throughput measured in gas per second. We only include EVM equivalent Layer 2 gas usage.


Value Locked

Stablecoin Supply: The sum of stablecoins that are locked on the chain.
Total Value Locked: The sum of all funds locked on the chain.


Economics

Application Revenue: The total amount of fees that applications charge their users per chain (this does not include gas fees). This metric shows how much value applications capture from users. 
Chain Revenue: The sum of fees that were paid by users of the chain in gas fees or, in the case of chains like Immutable X, the amount of fees that were paid to the protocol wallet.
Rent Paid to L1: The gas fees paid by L2s to post transaction data & verification states onto Ethereum.
Onchain Profit: The net profit of L2s, accounting for revenues as L2 gas fees collected and expenses as posting transaction data & verification states onto Ethereum.
Fully Diluted Valuation: The theoretical market cap of a token if all its planned tokens (total supply) were issued at the current price.
Market Cap: The total value of all circulating tokens, calculated by multiplying the current price of a single token by the total number of tokens in circulation.


Convenience

Transaction Costs: The median amount that is paid per transaction.

Since May 2023, we have been tracking Layer 2s building on Ethereum, now having Ethereum L1 and L2 data on 30+ different chains, including fundamental metrics, economics, blockspace usage, data availability metrics, and application metric data. All data is pulled by us using RPCs, indexed, cleaned and visualized on our platform. Furthermore, all data is free to access through our API (for example, ethereum.org is using our API for their Layer 2 data). For blockspace usage, we label smart contracts that are deployed on the different chains we are tracking, in order to better track different smart contracts by different projects on different chains in the Ethereum ecosystem. This allows for better traceability and understanding where usage lies and how different projects are performing on different chains. This also allows end users and researchers to see what is happening in the entire Ethereum ecosystem, and builders can prove their traction across different chains. For smart contract labelling, we adhere to the Open Labels Initiative standard, again allowing everyone to use this data and use this open dataset. Our aim has always been to unify Ethereum and its chains, showing transparently what is going on and how these different chains are not parasitic to each other, but grow the Ethereum pie as a whole.
