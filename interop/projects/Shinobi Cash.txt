Shinobi.cash: Borderless Privacy for a Multi-Chain World


Shinobi.cash is a cross-chain privacy protocol enabling users to deposit on one blockchain and withdraw privately on another ‚Äî with full cryptographic unlinkability between deposits and withdrawals.



By combining zero-knowledge proofs (ZK-SNARKs) with the Open Intent Framework (OIF), Shinobi.cash treats the entire multi-chain ecosystem as one unified privacy set.



Tagline: ‚ÄúBecause privacy shouldn‚Äôt stop at chain boundaries.‚Äù



The Problem



Public blockchains expose every transaction, linkable forever to wallet addresses. This leads to:

Transaction surveillance and deanonymization risks
Fragmented privacy sets ‚Äî privacy isolated per chain
Poor UX for users moving between chains


Existing privacy solutions like Tornado Cash or Privacy Pool work within a single chain, forcing users to either use bridge during cross-chain operations(Bad UX) or maintain separate privacy pools per chain‚Äîseverely limiting the anonymity set.



Our Solution



Zero-Knowledge Cross-Chain Privacy

Users deposit on Chain A (e.g., Base) and withdraw privately on Chain B (e.g., Arbitrum).
ZK-SNARKs prove ownership of deposits without revealing identities.
Nullifiers prevent double-spending.
Unified cross-chain pool expands anonymity set across all supported chains.
Decentralized Settlement via OIF

OIF enables intent-based, trustless cross-chain settlement:
Intent-based deposits: Solvers compete to fill user intents; verified by oracles.
Intent-based withdrawals: ZK proofs validate ownership; settlement verified by fill oracles.
No centralized relayers or bridges.
Transparency: All intents public ‚Äî privacy enforced only through ZK proofs.

OIF provides the infrastructure, not privacy itself ‚Äî enabling decentralized execution for ZK-based privacy.
