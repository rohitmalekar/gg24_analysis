### CONTRIBUTIONS

The Vocdoni project has contributed across four identified strategic domains: real-world adoption, research and development, free and open-source software, and privacy education and advocacy. These contributions collectively advance the state of digital democracy and verifiable governance infrastructure.

1.  Real-world and mainstream adoption (RWA) Since 2019, Vocdoni has deployed secure, end-to-end verifiable digital voting systems for a wide range of communities and organizations, including NGOs, political parties, sports clubs, cooperatives, and public institutions such as city halls. It has also empowered persecuted and exiled political movements, such as the New Belarus opposition, to hold censorship-resistant elections in restrictive environments.
2.  Research and Development (R&D) Vocdoni is actively advancing the scientific and technical frontiers of decentralized governance. Its research covers zero-knowledge proofs for ballot privacy and eligibility, verifiable tallying mechanisms, and hybrid architectures that combine off-chain scalability with on-chain verifiability.
3.  Free and open-source software (FOSS) Vocdoni's entire technology stack is open-source, fostering transparency, reusability, and community-driven innovation. By publicly releasing its protocols, SDKs, reference implementations, and developer tools, Vocdoni empowers organizations and DAOs worldwide to implement trustless, privacy-preserving governance systems. This open approach strengthens the global digital democracy ecosystem, ensuring that critical civic infrastructure remains auditable, non-proprietary, and sustainable.
4.  Privacy education and advocacy (PEA) Beyond technological development, Vocdoni is committed to educating communities about privacy, digital rights, and democratic participation. Through workshops, public talks, and accessible materials, the project raises awareness of data sovereignty and the ethical use of cryptography as enablers of trustworthy digital governance. These actions foster a culture of privacy literacy and civic empowerment, ensuring that digital democracy evolves alongside informed, privacy-conscious citizens.
