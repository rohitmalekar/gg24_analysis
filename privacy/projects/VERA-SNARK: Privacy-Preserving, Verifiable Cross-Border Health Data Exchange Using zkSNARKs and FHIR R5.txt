Every year, people die because their medical records cannot cross a border in time. A traveler collapses on vacation. A child needs a transplant in another country. A refugee seeks treatment but carries no documented history. In each story, a silent barrier stands between care and survival --- data silos.VERA-SNARK was born from this pain. It is not another blockchain prototype --- it is a *forensic intervention* into one of the most pressing humanitarian failures of our digital age: how to make health data useful without making it vulnerable.VERA-SNARK builds a Zero-Knowledge interoperability layer that allows hospitals to *verify* medical records without ever *revealing* them. Its cryptographic engine, powered by zkSNARKs, generates three verifiable proofs:

-   Authorization Proof --- that the requester is a verified medical institution
-   Consent Proof --- that the patient has explicitly approved this access
-   Minimization Proof --- that only the essential data is shared, nothing more

Together, these proofs make trust *programmable* --- enabling lawful, instant access to medical data under GDPR and the European Health Data Space (EHDS) framework.We are integrating this with FHIR R5, CEN EN 13606, and the EHDS architecture, ensuring that privacy-preserving interoperability becomes the European standard, not an exception.This is not theoretical research. We are building an MVP, a working model to let pilot hospitals in Europe securely exchange patient data during emergencies --- compliant with GDPR Articles 5 (Minimization), 6 (Lawful Basis), and 17 (Right to Erasure).Your support will help us:

-   Develop the triple-proof zkSNARK protocol
-   Build the first verifiable consent engine for Electronic Health Records (EHRs)
-   Launch pilot integrations with European healthcare systems
-   Open-source our libraries for developers, researchers, and public agencies

We are building a future where a doctor in Berlin can save a patient from Oslo --- because trust no longer needs paper; it has cryptography. VERA-SNARK: When privacy and life meet halfway, humanity wins.
