The Blockchain Socialist is a media platform founded by Joshua Dávila that explores the intersection of blockchain technology, digital privacy, and progressive politics. Dávila has produced hundreds of pieces of content through various mediums including writing, podcasts, video and in person presentation that have centered around privacy while nurturing an online community with thousands of members. The platform challenges the dominant libertarian narrative in cryptocurrency spaces by demonstrating how decentralized technologies can serve collective liberation, economic justice, and privacy protection from a left-wing perspective. He is also the author of "Blockchain Radicals: How Capitalism Ruined Crypto and How to Fix It" (2023) published by Repeater Books."An important complement to existing narratives about the technology." - Vitalik Buterin on Blockchain RadicalsMission & ImpactThe platform addresses a critical gap in cryptocurrency discourse by providing accessible, rigorous analysis that connects blockchain technology to broader social and political concerns. Through podcasts, written analysis, video essays, and conference presentations, The Blockchain Socialist has become a key voice for understanding how privacy-preserving technologies can resist surveillance capitalism and support democratic, cooperative economic models in a way that is understandable to non-technical audiences.The platform has established itself as a leading voice on privacy from a progressive perspective, reframing privacy not as an individualist concern but as essential infrastructure for social movements and collective action.

### CONTRIBUTIONS

Joshua Dávila has made substantial contributions to privacy advocacy and education in the blockchain space and elsewhere. His work spans multiple formats and audiences, consistently exploring how privacy-preserving technologies can serve a progressive politics.Through his podcast, Dávila has produced extensive content covering critical privacy infrastructure including mixnets (Nym), anonymous DeFi networks (DarkFi), decentralized identity systems that avoid biometric surveillance (Holonym, Idena), privacy preservation mechanisms (Privacy Pools), censorship-resistant voting protocols (Vocdoni, Aragon), and decentralized alternatives to surveillance platforms (Fileverse). His conversations with figures like Amir Taaki and Harry Halpin have explored why anonymity and privacy are fundamental to democratic organizing.He has given conference presentations that directly address the relationship between privacy and solidarity. At the Web3Privacy Now Berlin Meetup in 2024, he explored "The tension between privacy and solidarity," and presented at the Web3Privacy Now track at Dappcon 2025 outlining a framework for solidarity primitives. He led the panel for "Turning Degens Into Cypherpunks" panel at the Ethereum Cypherpunk Congress in Bangkok demonstrating his commitment to shifting crypto culture toward privacy consciousness.Through his book "Blockchain Radicals: How Capitalism Ruined Crypto and How to Fix It," Dávila provides a comprehensive critique and vision for how privacy and decentralization can serve progressive political goals, expanding the discourse beyond typical libertarian framings to demonstrate privacy's essential role in resisting surveillance capitalism and enabling grassroots organizing.
