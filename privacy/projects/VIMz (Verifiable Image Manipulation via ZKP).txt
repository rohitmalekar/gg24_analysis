*THE PROBLEM*Ensuring that images shared online are authentic and trustworthy is increasingly difficult. Even genuine photos often require edits before publication --- cropping, redaction, or color correction --- yet these legitimate modifications blur the line between honest adjustments and malicious manipulations such as AI-generated or doctored content. proprietary tools (e.g., Adobe) and trust closed-source authorities to manage signing keys securely. Beyond the centralization risk, this model exposes the entire creative process, threatening the privacy of photographers, editors, and subjects.However, a fundamentally different approach is also possible.*APPLICATIONS*

-   Trustless, GDPR-compliant media marketplaces
-   Authentic AI dataset verification
-   KYC: Privacy-preserving liveness and personhood proofs
-   Trustworthy but Anonymous journalism/photography

*THE SOLUTION*Using zero-knowledge proofs (ZKPs), we can potentially verify an image's authenticity and editing process without revealing originals or intermediates. But the challenge has always been scalability: ZKPs are computationally expensive over large data arrays like high-resolution images. Especially when considering our setting, an image editor or a photographer that wants to have everything always in the client-side. No server/cloud can be trusted with such sensitive media.VIMz solves this by leveraging folding-based zkSNARKs (like Nova), achieving efficient proof generation for real-world image sizes. It can verify that both original and final images are legitimate and that transformations (crop, resize, tone mapping, etc.) were applied correctly, all while keeping data private. Proofs remain under 11 KB, verification takes less than one second, and 8K (33 MP) images can be proven on a mid-range laptop, with only 10 GB of RAM requirement.
